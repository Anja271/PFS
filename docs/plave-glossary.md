## Age-language relationships

Use this only to validate a spoken form of address. Never add a relationship term that was not spoken.

- Bamby may address Yejun and Noah as `hyung`, `Yejun-hyung`, or `Noah-hyung`.
- Eunho and Hamin may address Bamby as `hyung` or `Bonggu-hyung` when the Korean contains `형`.
- Eunho may address Yejun and Noah as `hyung`.
- Hamin may address the older members as `hyung`.

Hard prohibition: never render `형` as `bro`, `brother`, `big brother`, or `Mr.`.

## Translation lessons from video `99nR8UeN76M`

These are scene-specific precedents, not universal facts for unrelated videos:

- A caller can imitate another member's voice. In this video, Eunho initially pretended to be Noah. Do not assign a speaker from vocal style alone; follow later identification and conversation continuity.
- Noah's fitness interest helped confirm a heavily damaged first-person subject in a workout exchange, but personality traits are supporting context, never standalone proof.
- Yejun's line at `01:34:54.000` referred to Bamby's Weverse post from the previous day making Bamby appear upset. Temporal attachments in damaged ASR must be checked against the entire exchange.
- `ddudori`, `japdori`, and `nokdori` were retained as cautious romanizations during explicit wordplay rather than collapsed into one English term.
- When only Shawn Mendes was reliably identifiable in a two-song comparison, the other title was omitted rather than guessed.

## Speaker and context discipline

- Names, hobbies, running jokes, and known relationships may resolve ambiguity only when they agree with grammar and adjacent dialogue.
- Never turn glossary context into dialogue.
- Never explain an insider inside the VTT unless a speaker explains it.
- Record uncertain names and references in the review report.
- Extend this glossary only with a source and a short note describing why the term is reliable.
